Patterns across the market

  1. The minimal winning feature set is small and consistent: shared WhatsApp inbox → contacts auto-created from inbound chats (matched by phone) → kanban pipeline with drag-and-drop stages → tags / custom fields / notes → tasks + follow-up reminders → message templates / quick replies → auto-assignment of chats to reps. Callbell wins with even less (no deal amounts, no tasks module). Nobody clones Pipedrive.
  2. Dual connection wins; QR-session is the adoption path. Kommo, Leadsales, Pepper Cloud, Agendor, TeleCRM, SocialHub all ship QR/WhatsApp-Web as the low-friction entry AND official WABA above it. Privyr got to 500k businesses personal-number-first, "no WhatsApp API needed". WABA-only players (Interakt, Callbell) carry setup friction + per-conversation Meta fees + seat minimums — high effective entry cost.
  3. Pricing anchors: per-seat $6–25/mo, or per-workspace bundles $18–97/mo with seat caps. The WhatsApp layer is frequently a separate paid add-on (Agendor R$49/number, TeleCRM ~$15/seat chat-sync, Privyr's automations, Kommo's WABA fees) — SMBs demonstrably pay extra specifically for WhatsApp-in-CRM.
  4. Positioning: don't lead with the word "CRM". Kommo rebranded away from "amoCRM" in 2022 because "CRM" was "unknown jargon that pushed away" messaging-first users. The pitch that works: "run your sales inside WhatsApp", not "adopt a CRM".
  5. AI is the standard upsell layer: conversation summaries, suggested replies, no-code bots/qualifiers, and — most interesting — Moskit's "Smart Fields" auto-extracting CRM field values from WhatsApp conversations; Pepper Cloud's AssistAI "chats into won deals". Consistent with layering AI on top of the base CRM.
  6. Demand is proven at every price point — from 500k-user platforms down to $0 indie Chrome extensions over WhatsApp Web (WAPlus, WA Lead Pilot, WhatsApp Web CRM™, WaBaCRM, Lion CRM) that ship pipeline+tags+notes+reminders with no backend at all.

Product cards

Kommo (ex-amoCRM) — the category king

"#1 AI CRM for messenger-based sales": unified WhatsApp/IG/Telegram inbox feeding a kanban pipeline, Salesbot moves leads between stages. Dual WhatsApp: free "WhatsApp Lite" (QR, personal or Business-app number) even on Base, official Cloud API for templates/broadcasts. Features: pipelines, contacts, tasks, notes, calendar, automation, broadcasts, booking; AI summaries/replies/agents. $15/25/45 per seat, but only 6–24-month upfront billing — a self-serve monthly plan undercuts it. Lesson: minimal set + dual WhatsApp + drop "CRM" from the language.

Privyr — closest to our no-CRM user

Mobile-first CRM for individual B2C salespeople who live on their own WhatsApp number: instant lead alerts from FB/IG/TikTok/Google ads → one-tap templated, auto-personalised WhatsApp follow-up → simple pipeline stages → activity timeline. No WABA needed for the core; official-API auto-responder/campaigns sold as add-ons. Free tier, $25/seat annual. Claims 500k+ businesses across 125 countries. Lesson: the "salesperson who will never open a desktop CRM" persona is winnable at scale off the personal number — exactly our 80%.

SocialHub — Brazil, pipeline wrapped around the inbox

"CRM, Chatbot e Automação de WhatsApp em uma só plataforma"; CRM bundled free in every plan, kanban funnel is the same screen as the chat. Dual QR + official BSP; up to 50 agents on one number. R$99/199/399 per workspace (~$18–74). 15k+ companies. Lesson: workspace pricing with bundled seats+numbers; CRM as free wrapper drives the messaging subscription.

Leadsales — LATAM, deliberately simple

Conversational CRM: WhatsApp+FB+IG into one shared inbox, chats as cards on kanban "embudos", auto-assignment, scheduled follow-ups. QR entry / WABA higher tiers with conversation credits. $97/mo workspace (3 users, +$13/user). 2.8k+ companies, $4.3M raised. Lesson: volume-tiered workspace pricing (conversations/funnels/storage), not per-seat.

Interakt — India, WABA-only counterexample

Jio Haptik's WhatsApp sales CRM: real pipeline depth (stages, unified contact view, tasks, custom fields, round-robin, role-based access, stage-drop-off analytics). WABA-only, per-conversation Meta fees on top, ~$30/mo floor (5 seats). 10k+ businesses. Lesson: proves WhatsApp-first CRM sells at SMB scale in India; its lack of a personal/QR path is the gap we'd exploit.

Callbell — just-enough CRM around the inbox

Multi-agent inbox (WA/IG/Messenger/Telegram) + the lightest possible funnel: named stages picked from within the chat, contacts with custom fields/tags/notes, round-robin, broadcasts, stats. WABA-only, min 3 seats, WhatsApp consumption add-on €50 — real entry cost far above the €15 headline. Lesson: validates shipping a simple pipeline, not a Pipedrive clone; its pricing friction is our opening.

Moskit CRM — Brazil, the Boost model

Proper SMB sales CRM (contacts, funnel, tasks, proposals, reports) whose native WhatsApp is a first-party Chrome extension riding the user's WhatsApp Web session: auto-syncs text/audio/images/docs into the deal timeline every ~2 min, matches contacts by phone, surfaces deal data + templates inside WhatsApp Web. AI: Smart Fields extracts CRM field values from chats; Smart AI writes/summarizes. R$89–179/seat; Boost is a paid add-on. Lesson: the QR-session sync into a deal timeline is exactly our existing tech; field auto-extraction from chats is the AI feature to copy.

Agendor — Brazil, WhatsApp as paid add-on

Mature B2B SMB CRM (funnels, deals, tasks, goals, mobile-first for field reps). Two WhatsApp products: native "Agendor Chat" inbox (Cloud API or Gupshup, auto-creates contacts from inbound messages, create/view deals without leaving the chat, stage-change fires automated messages) and "WhatsApp Sync" Chrome extension (QR). CRM R$59/seat; Chat R$312/mo (3 users) or R$93/user; Sync R$49/number. ~6k payers, R$18.5M revenue 2025. Lesson: per-number add-on monetization + the auto-loop (inbound msg → contact → deal from chat → auto-message on stage change) is the core UX to ship.

TeleCRM — India, telecalling + WhatsApp

Indian MSME CRM: lead capture from 15+ sources (IndiaMART, Justdial, portals, ads), pipeline, auto-dialer with call recording, follow-ups. WhatsApp dual: WABA (one-time ₹1,999 setup, no platform surcharge) + "Chat Sync" Chrome extension capturing the whole team's WhatsApp into one dashboard (~$15/seat add-on). ~$10/seat. 2.5k+ businesses (Domino's, BYJU'S logos). Lesson: low seat price + WhatsApp layer as the paid add-on; multi-source lead capture matters in lead-gen-heavy markets.

Pepper Cloud — SEA, dual-path in a real CRM

Singapore SME sales CRM (pipelines, opportunities, forecasting, tickets, dashboards) with both WhatsApp paths native: QR ("without API costs, approvals or setup", unlimited accounts) + Cloud API for templates/broadcasts/AssistAI bot. Bootstrapped to ~$4.7M revenue. Counter-signal: pricing fully demo-gated, sales-led — the opposite of the self-serve motion our segment needs.
